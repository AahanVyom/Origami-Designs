Origami Instructions & Diagrams

Welcome to our origami collection! Here, you'll find step-by-step instructions to craft 8 amazing animal figures. Simply click on the image of any origami to be redirected to detailed diagrams and easy-to-follow steps for creating your own paper masterpiece. Let the folding fun begin!

Camel

  • Camel's ears are furry
  • Camels can move easily across the sand because of their specially designed feet
  • When they find water, they will drink as much as possible

Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities

Teddy Bear

  • Teddy bears benefit children by reducing anxiety and stress
  • Teddy bears have a depth of character that other toys lack
  • Many adults still have fond memories of their childhood teddy bears

Panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim
  • Pandas are adored for their cuteness
  • Pandas spend a lot of their day eating bamboo, which is critical to their diet

Frog

  • Frogs have existed for over 200 million years, even alongside dinosaurs
  • There are over 6,000 species of frogs worldwide
  • The word "toad" typically refers to frogs with warty, dry skin and shorter hind legs

Flying Cicada

  • Cicadas have been used as money in folk medicine and to forecast the weather
  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies
  • Most cicadas have red-orange eyes

Rabbit

  • A female rabbit is called a 'doe' and a male rabbit is called a 'buck'
  • Bunnies will sometimes jump around and flick their heads and feet to express happiness
  • Rabbit ears can rotate up to 270 degrees
